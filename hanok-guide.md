      <h1 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Hanok Village Guide</h1>
    </header>

    <p>
      Jeonju Hanok Village is not a place you rush through.
      Its 700-plus traditional houses, narrow earthen lanes, and centuries-old temples
      reward those who arrive without an itinerary and leave without looking at the time.
      This is our quiet guide — the places we love, the paths worth taking slowly.
    </p>

    <hr class="major" />

    <h2 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Where to Begin</h2>

    <div class="row">
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Namcheon Bridge (남천교)</h3>
        <p>
          Step outside our doors and you are already there.
          The bridge frames a perfect view of the village at dusk —
          when the hanok rooftiles catch the last of the light and the creek runs quietly below.
          A good place to start, and an equally good place to end.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Gyeonggijeon Shrine (경기전)</h3>
        <p>
          A 15th-century royal shrine housing the portrait of King Taejo, founder of the Joseon Dynasty.
          The ginkgo trees along the entrance path are among the oldest in the region.
          Arrive early — the stillness of the morning here is something apart.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Jeondong Catholic Cathedral (전동성당)</h3>
        <p>
          Built in 1914 on the site of Korea's first Catholic martyrdom, this Byzantine-Romanesque cathedral
          stands in quiet contrast to the hanok rooflines that surround it.
          One of the most photographed buildings in Jeonju — for good reason.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Omokdae Pavilion (오목대)</h3>
        <p>
          A short climb above the village rewards you with the finest panoramic view of the roofscape.
          Best visited at golden hour, when the entire village seems to exhale at once.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <hr class="major" />

    <h2 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Getting Here</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Address:</strong> 66, Jeonjucheondong-ro, Wansan-gu, Jeonju-si, Jeonbuk-do</li>
      <li><strong>From Jeonju Station:</strong> Bus 79 or 537 → Hanok Village stop (approx. 20 min)</li>
      <li><strong>From Jeonju Bus Terminal:</strong> Bus 119 or taxi (approx. 15 min)</li>
      <li><strong>Parking:</strong> Public lots available near the village entrance. Weekends fill early.</li>
    </ul>

    <hr class="major" />

    <h2 style="color: #E6D5B8;">A Few Quiet Suggestions</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>The village before 9 AM belongs almost entirely to you. Come early.</li>
      <li>Wear shoes you don't mind on cobblestone. The best lanes are unpaved.</li>
      <li>Most shops open around 10 AM. The cafés, slightly later.</li>
      <li>If it rains, stay. The hanok village in the rain is a different, quieter thing.</li>
    </ul>
