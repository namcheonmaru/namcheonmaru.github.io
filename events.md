      <h1 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Performances & Events</h1>
    </header>

    <p>
      The village is never entirely still. Throughout the year, Jeonju Hanok Village hosts
      pansori recitals, mask dance performances, lantern festivals, and seasonal ceremonies
      that have been observed here for centuries.
      Below is a guide to what you might encounter — and what is worth planning around.
    </p>

    <hr class="major" />

    <h2 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Recurring Performances</h2>

    <div class="row">
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Pansori (판소리)</h3>
        <p>
          Korea's UNESCO-listed narrative singing tradition.
          Performances are held regularly at Gyeonggijeon Shrine and the Hanok Village Cultural Center.
          A single vocalist and a drummer carry an entire story — sometimes for hours.
          No prior knowledge required. It moves you regardless.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Talchum — Mask Dance (탈춤)</h3>
        <p>
          A traditional performance combining dance, music, and satire.
          Rooted in folk tradition, it was once the art of the common people.
          Performances are held on weekends near the village entrance plaza.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Haegeum & Gayageum Recitals</h3>
        <p>
          Chamber performances of traditional Korean string instruments,
          often held in intimate hanok courtyards.
          Check the Jeonju Cultural Foundation schedule for upcoming dates.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-6 col-12-small">
        <h3>Hanbok Experience (한복 체험)</h3>
        <p>
          Rental shops throughout the village offer hanbok for the day.
          Many visitors find that wearing one changes how the village feels entirely.
          Recommended especially for first-time visitors.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <hr class="major" />

    <h2 style="color: #E6D5B8;">Seasonal Highlights</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Jeonju International Film Festival (JIFF)</strong> — Late April to early May. One of Korea's most respected independent film festivals, held partly within the village.</li>
      <li><strong>Jeonju Hanok Village Lantern Festival</strong> — Autumn. The village is lit by hundreds of traditional lanterns after dark. A rare sight.</li>
      <li><strong>Daeboreum (대보름)</strong> — First full moon of the lunar new year. Traditional games, fire festivals, and ritual ceremonies throughout the village.</li>
      <li><strong>Chuseok (추석) Celebrations</strong> — Korean harvest festival. Special performances and ancestral rites observed at Gyeonggijeon and surrounding shrines.</li>
    </ul>
